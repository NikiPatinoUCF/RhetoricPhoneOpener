Activity 03 — The Toolkit
RHETORIC'S
BIG THREE

Tap each card to reveal its definition.

ETHOS
Credibility appeal — the author establishes trust or authority.
PATHOS
Emotional appeal — the text targets your feelings to persuade.
LOGOS
Logical appeal — evidence, data, and reason drive the argument.
KAIROS
Timing — the text exploits a specific moment to be persuasive.
AUDIENCE
Who the text is written FOR — shapes every rhetorical choice.
PURPOSE
What the text is trying to DO — inform, persuade, entertain?
CONTEXT
The situation surrounding the text — cultural, historical, social.
EXIGENCE
The urgency or problem that called the text into being.

What Critical Analysis Does

Analysis writing doesn't just ask "what does the text say?" It asks how and why — how is the text constructed to persuade, and why does that construction work (or fail) on a particular audience in a particular moment?

For Your Post (Exit Ticket)

  • What is the exigence — why was this made now?
  • Who is the intended audience?
  • Identify one rhetorical appeal (ethos/pathos/logos).
  • Is there anything the text leaves out or glosses over?
